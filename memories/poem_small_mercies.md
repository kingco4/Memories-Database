---
title: Small Mercies
type: poem
date: 2020-05-03
tags: pandemic, stillness, gratitude, spring, isolation
---

The neighbor's cat has been sleeping
in the planter box all week —
a gray parenthesis
curled around the not-yet-blooming.

I watch her from the window.
She does not know about any of this.

The bread I baked yesterday
was ugly and dense
and I ate it warm with butter
standing over the sink
and it was the best thing
that had happened to me in weeks.

A mercy: the way light moves
across a room in the morning.

A mercy: the sound of birds
who also did not get the news.

I am learning to receive
the small things
without apology —
to let the warmth of bread
and the indifferent grace of cats
be enough,

at least
for today.
