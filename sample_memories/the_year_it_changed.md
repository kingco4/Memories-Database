---
title: The Year Everything Quietly Shifted
type: journal
date: 2022-03-01
tags: change, uncertainty, transition, self, identity
---

I can't point to a day. That's the thing about it. There was no morning I woke up different. It was more like a ship that has been turning for so long that you only realize it's facing a new direction when you finally look up.

Somewhere between January and now I stopped wanting the things I used to want. The career I'd been building for eight years started to feel like a costume I'd forgotten I was wearing. The apartment I loved started to feel like someone else's idea of who I should be.

I've been walking a lot. Long walks with no destination, which is new. Before, I always needed a destination. I am learning that I walk differently when I'm not trying to get somewhere — slower, more likely to stop, more likely to look up at buildings.

I called my old friend M. last week. We hadn't spoken in three years. She sounded exactly the same, and I told her so, and she laughed and said "You don't." She meant it as an observation, not a judgment. I think I've changed in a way that's visible now. That's new too.

I don't know yet if what's happening is loss or arrival. Maybe that's the wrong frame. Maybe it's just motion. The river doesn't mourn the bends it takes.

I'm trying to stop waiting for the version of myself who has it figured out. She might not be coming. What's here instead might be enough.
